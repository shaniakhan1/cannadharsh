The Arc

A second act,
earned slowly.

Born in Sri Lanka and raised through displacement, Dharsh built her career the traditional way first. Toronto. Chartered accounting at KPMG. Thirteen years at PepsiCo, climbing into leadership roles inside one of the most disciplined operating cultures in the world.

Then, in 2018, cannabis stopped being someone else's conversation. As a medical patient in New Jersey, Dharsh watched the plant do what corporate wellness programs had spent years trying to promise. Something shifted.

"The typical narratives around cannabis don't capture its essence, or its potential. My work is to change that."

By 2023, she had earned her certification through the Ganjier program, the industry's most rigorous sommelier-style credential. That same year, she co-founded Joyleaf, the only dispensary in New Jersey that lets customers actually touch the product, browse the shelves, and make informed decisions without shame.

Now she speaks to rooms that once looked like the ones she came from. Boardrooms. Industry panels. Newsrooms. The message hasn't changed since Sri Lanka: know what you're working with, and treat it with respect.
